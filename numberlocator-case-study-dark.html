Number Locator

Smart Caller Identification

A mobile utility app that empowers users to identify unknown callers and track phone numbers — putting control back in the hands of the user.

UI/UX Designer
Timeline
2 Weeks

Project Overview

Unwanted calls, spam, and scams are a growing problem for smartphone users worldwide. Most people have no way of knowing who is calling before they pick up — leading to frustration, wasted time, and even financial loss from scam calls.

Number Locator was designed to give users complete visibility into who is contacting them. The app combines real-time caller identification with phone number lookup, all wrapped in a clean, trustworthy interface that anyone can use effortlessly.

The Problem

Through competitive analysis and user research, I identified several core pain points that existing caller ID apps fail to address:

  • Unknown Callers: Users constantly receive calls from unrecognized numbers with no way to identify them before answering
  • Cluttered Interfaces: Existing apps are overloaded with ads and unnecessary features that make simple lookups frustrating
  • Lack of Trust Signals: Users feel uncertain about the reliability of caller data shown to them
  • Poor Search Experience: Manually looking up numbers across multiple apps is time-consuming and inconsistent

Target Audience

Primary Users: Everyday smartphone users (ages 20–55) who frequently receive calls from unknown numbers and want a fast, reliable way to identify callers before picking up.

Secondary Users: Small business owners and professionals who need to verify contact numbers and manage incoming communications more effectively.

Design Process

1. Research & Discovery

I began by analyzing leading caller ID and number lookup apps — including Truecaller, Hiya, and CallApp — to understand common interaction patterns, identify UX gaps, and define what "smart" caller identification truly means to users.

Key research insights: users want identification results instantly, they prefer a clean result screen over feature-heavy dashboards, and trust indicators like verified badges and spam flags are critical to user confidence.

2. Information Architecture

I mapped out the core user flows to create a logical and frictionless navigation structure:

  • Splash Screen → Onboarding (3 steps) → Home Dashboard
  • Home → Number Lookup → Identification Result
  • Dialer → Recents → Contacts → Settings
  • Free Tier → Paywall → Premium Subscription

3. Splash Screen

The splash screen immediately establishes the app's identity — the bold app icon with the phone + search symbol communicates the core function at a glance. A loading bar keeps users informed that the app is initializing in the background.

4. Onboarding Flow

The three-screen onboarding introduces users to the app's core value propositions in a simple, progressive format. Each screen focuses on a single message with a clear icon, a bold headline, and a supporting subtitle — keeping cognitive load low. The dot indicator and Next/Get Started buttons create a smooth, guided flow.

Key Features & Screens

Home Screen – Number Lookup

The home screen is built around the app's core action: looking up a phone number. A prominent search field at the bottom of the screen invites users to enter any number, while an illustrative visual reinforces the lookup concept. The bottom navigation bar provides quick access to Dialer, Recents, Lookup, Contacts, and Settings — keeping all key features one tap away.

Paywall – Upgrade to Premium

The paywall screen presents three subscription tiers clearly and transparently — Weekly, Monthly, and 3-Month plans — each with savings badges to reinforce value. Trust signals including "Cancel Anytime," "No Hidden Charges," and "Secure Payments" address common objections directly. The premium crown badge and benefit checklist quickly communicate what users gain by upgrading.

Design Decisions

Text & Depth

Why Blue? Blue is universally associated with trust, security, and reliability — precisely the emotions users need to feel when relying on caller identification. The clean white background keeps the interface feeling light and non-threatening, while the blue accents draw attention to the key actions.

Typography & Hierarchy

Bold, dark headlines on a white background create instant readability. Supporting subtitles use a lighter weight to establish hierarchy without competing with the primary message. CTAs use white text on solid blue buttons to create unmissable call-to-action contrast.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1

Problem: Communicating the app's full value in a 3-screen onboarding without overwhelming users

Solution 1

Each onboarding screen focuses on exactly one message — Welcome, Features, Privacy — with a single icon, headline, and one-line description. Simple dot pagination lets users feel progress without distraction

Challenge 2

Problem: Designing a paywall that converts without feeling pushy or untrustworthy

Solution 2

Used transparent pricing with savings badges, clear benefit lists, and three trust signals (Cancel Anytime, No Hidden Charges, Secure Payments) directly above the subscribe button to reduce friction at the moment of decision

Challenge 3

Problem: Making the home screen feel purposeful even before a number is searched

Solution 3

Used an illustrative visual of people being searched through a magnifying glass to reinforce the app's purpose visually, making the empty state feel intentional rather than incomplete

Design Principles

Clarity First

Every screen communicates one primary action. No cluttered menus or competing calls-to-action distract from what the user needs to do next

Trust Through Design

Blue color language, verified indicators, and transparent pricing all work together to make users feel safe using the app with their personal call data

Frictionless Onboarding

Three focused screens get users to the core experience fast, with a Skip option respecting users who want to jump straight in

Honest Monetization

The paywall is transparent about pricing, savings, and cancellation — building long-term trust rather than tricking users into subscriptions

Key Takeaways

  • One message per screen: The onboarding's single-focus approach keeps users engaged and informed without overwhelming them
  • Empty states matter: A well-designed empty home screen sets expectations and encourages first use rather than leaving users confused
  • Paywalls need trust: Conversion improves when users feel safe — transparent pricing and trust badges do as much as the features list
  • Color is a UX tool: The consistent blue palette isn't just aesthetic — it actively communicates security and reliability throughout the app

Next Steps

  • Design the caller ID overlay that appears during an incoming call with real-time identification
  • Build out the Recents and Contacts screens with detailed call logs and identification history

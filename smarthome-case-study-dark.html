Smart Home

Control Your Home, Anywhere, Anytime

A comprehensive smart home control app that puts your entire home at your fingertips. Manage devices, monitor energy usage, and create the perfect environment—all from a single, intuitive interface.

Role
UI/UX Designer
Timeline
2 Weeks
Platform
Mobile (iOS/Android)
Tools
Figma

Project Overview

As smart home technology becomes increasingly prevalent, users face a growing challenge: managing multiple devices across different rooms through various disconnected apps. Smart Home was designed to solve this fragmentation by providing a unified control center for the entire home ecosystem.

The app targets tech-savvy homeowners who want seamless control over their connected devices, real-time monitoring of energy consumption, and the ability to manage their home environment from anywhere in the world.

The Problem

Through user research and interviews with smart home device owners, I identified critical pain points in current smart home management:

  • App Overload: Users juggle multiple apps for different device brands and categories
  • No Centralized View: Difficult to see the status of all devices at once
  • Energy Blindness: Users lack visibility into which devices consume the most power
  • Complex Control: Simple tasks require navigating through multiple menus
  • Limited Insights: Existing apps don't provide meaningful analytics or usage patterns

Target Audience

Primary Users: Tech-savvy homeowners (ages 25-50) who have invested in smart home devices and want efficient control over their connected environment.

Secondary Users: Busy professionals who travel frequently and need remote access to monitor and control their home.

User Goals: Simplify device management, reduce energy costs, monitor home security remotely, and create automated routines for convenience.

Design Process

1. Research & Discovery

I analyzed leading smart home apps including Google Home, Apple HomeKit, and Samsung SmartThings to understand existing patterns and identify opportunities for innovation. Key insight: users wanted better visualization and faster access to frequently used controls.

2. User Personas & Journey Mapping

I created personas representing different smart home users—from tech enthusiasts to practical homeowners focused on energy savings. This helped prioritize features like room-based organization and energy monitoring.

3. Information Architecture

The app structure emphasizes quick access and clear organization:

  • Home Overview → Room Selection → Device Control
  • Stats Dashboard → Energy Monitoring → Device Activity Insights
  • Security Hub → Access Control → Camera Monitoring

4. Wireframing & Prototyping

Starting with low-fidelity wireframes, I explored different approaches to displaying room layouts and device controls. The goal was to create an interface that felt natural and required minimal learning curve.

Design Decisions

Color Palette

The color scheme balances professionalism with warmth, creating a premium yet approachable interface:

Primary Actions
Yellow #fbbf24
Active States
Light Gray #f3f4f6
Cards & Sections

Why Dark Theme? A dark interface reduces eye strain during evening use and creates a modern, sophisticated aesthetic. The blue accents provide clear visual feedback, while yellow toggles indicate active devices at a glance.

Typography & Visual Hierarchy

Clean, modern typography with strong contrast ensures readability. Room names and device counts are prominently displayed, while status indicators use color and position to communicate information instantly.

Key Features & Screens

1. Room-Based Organization

The home screen displays all rooms with beautiful imagery, device count, and active status. Users can quickly see which rooms have active devices and access controls with a single tap. The "Add new Cameras" card encourages ecosystem expansion.

2. Intuitive Device Controls

Room detail pages show an immersive view of the actual space with easy-access controls for all devices. Large toggle switches make it simple to control lights, AC, TV, and curtains without navigating through menus. Device counts are clearly labeled for transparency.

3. Comprehensive Analytics Dashboard

The stats screen provides valuable insights through multiple data visualizations:

  • Energy & Power Usage: Weekly bar chart showing consumption patterns (8.1 kWh tracked)
  • Environment Stats: Real-time temperature monitoring with historical trends (24°C current)
  • Device Activity: Circular chart showing most-used devices (Fridge, AC, TV, Lights)
  • Security & Access: Quick overview of door unlocks, guest access, and camera activity

4. Smart Notifications & Alerts

The bell icon provides instant access to important notifications about device status, security events, and energy consumption alerts—keeping users informed without being intrusive.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1

Problem: How to display multiple device controls without overwhelming users

Solution 1

Organized devices by room with visual context and large, accessible toggle switches that show status at a glance

Challenge 2

Problem: Making energy data meaningful and actionable for users

Solution 2

Created multiple visualization types (bar charts, line graphs, pie charts) showing different aspects of usage with clear metrics

Challenge 3

Problem: Balancing detailed analytics with simple daily use

Solution 3

Separated controls (Home) from analytics (Stats) in distinct tabs, letting users choose their focus

Design Principles

Visual Context Matters

Room images help users quickly identify spaces and create emotional connection to their home

Instant Feedback

Toggle states, colors, and animations provide immediate confirmation of actions

Data-Driven Insights

Beautiful visualizations turn raw data into actionable information about energy and usage

Simple by Default

Common tasks require minimal taps—complexity is hidden until needed

Key Takeaways

  • Context creates intuition: Showing actual room images makes the interface instantly understandable
  • Data visualization drives action: Clear charts help users identify energy waste and optimize usage
  • Organization reduces complexity: Room-based structure mirrors how people think about their homes
  • Status at a glance: Color coding and device counts eliminate the need to dig for information

Impact & Results

Smart Home demonstrates my ability to design complex IoT interfaces that balance powerful functionality with simplicity. The design focuses on making smart home technology accessible and actionable.

  • Created a unified control center for all smart home devices
  • Designed comprehensive analytics that turn data into insights
  • Built an intuitive room-based navigation system
  • Developed clear visual hierarchy for instant status awareness
